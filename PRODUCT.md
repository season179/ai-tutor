# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Students, parents, and tutors use this on a wide desktop monitor while working through homework aloud. They need to start a realtime voice session, share a problem image, ask a focused question, and monitor connection or payload events without losing the thread of the lesson.

## Product Purpose

AI Tutor is a small Realtime API teaching room. It helps a learner talk through homework step by step, optionally attach an image of the problem, and receive concise spoken guidance from a patient tutor. Success means the user can start, share, and recover from errors quickly while staying calm enough to keep thinking.

## Brand Personality

Calm, precise, grounded. The interface should feel like a quiet study desk with useful instruments close at hand, not a marketing page, gaming console, or clinical admin tool.

## Anti-references

Avoid bright light themes, heavy dark themes, warm paper or cream palettes, decorative hero layouts, brass accents, glass panels, large gradients, ornamental card grids, and narrow mobile-first composition. The current warm paper treatment is not the target direction.

## Design Principles

- Keep the lesson in view: session actions, image context, and event diagnostics should all be visible together on a wide monitor.
- Use color as environment, not decoration: a middle-value zen green should carry the room while contrast and state colors do the functional work.
- Make states legible at a glance: ready, connecting, preparing, disabled, and error states need obvious affordances without alarmist styling.
- Preserve focus: dense enough for desktop work, quiet enough for evening use, with no page-load theater or needless motion.
- Favor familiar controls: standard buttons, file inputs, text areas, and logs should feel trustworthy and predictable.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast for text and controls. Maintain visible focus states, disabled states that remain understandable, reduced-motion behavior for any animation, and color choices that do not rely on green alone to communicate status.
